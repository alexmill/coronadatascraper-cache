Latest news

Sunday 22 March - Confirmation of ACT schools response to COVID-19

ACT schools, government and non-government, will begin pupil-free days from Tuesday 24 March through to the school holidays despite statements from the Prime Minister.

22 March 2020 - Statement: ACT Chief Minister

Following similar announcements from the Premier of Victoria and the Premier of New South Wales, I will also be informing National Cabinet that the ACT will proceed to a more comprehensive shutdown of non-essential services over the next 48 hours. I expect all states and territories will do the same.

22 March 2020 - Lyneham High School will be closed tomorrow 

A case of COVID-19 has been identified in the ACT as connected to Lyneham High School. Lyneham High School will be closed from tomorrow, Monday 23 March, for all students and staff.

22 March 2020 - COVID-19 testing criteria expanded 

The ACT is working to align with NSW in expanding the criteria for COVID-19 testing. These new criteria will apply from this afternoon, Sunday 22 March 2020.

22 March 2020 - COVID-19 update

The ACT Chief Health Officer, Dr Kerryn Coleman, has announced 10 new cases of COVID-19, bringing the ACT’s total to 19. For further information see our latest news page.

 

For all news updates see the Latest News page.

For general information on COVID-19 call the Coronavirus Health Information Line on 1800 020 080. This line operates 24 hours a day, seven days a week.

Feeling Unwell?

Seek medical attention if you develop a fever, cough, sore throat or shortness of breath:

  • within 14 days of overseas travel
  • following contact with someone who has COVID-19
  • if you’re a healthcare worker with direct patient contact – even if you have not recently travelled or had contact with someone who has COVID-19 

Call your GP

Visit the Weston Creek Walk-in Centre

Call Healthdirect on 1800 022 222

In an emergency, call triple zero (000).

Visit our Getting tested page for further advice and testing criteria.
